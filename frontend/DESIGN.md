## Brand & Style

The design system is crafted for **Ordem e Conselho Fraternal de Ministros Evangélico do Brasil** (OCOMEBE) to project an image of institutional stability, spiritual authority, and communal warmth. The full institutional name must be prominently displayed in key touchpoints (hero section, footer, legal pages). The acronym "OCOMEBE" may be used in navigation and compact contexts, but the full name takes precedence in headers, hero banners, and official communications. The target audience includes pastoral leaders, theological students, and congregants seeking a reliable source of ecclesiastical guidance and unity.

The aesthetic follows a **Corporate / Modern** style with **Minimalist** influences. It prioritizes clarity and dignity, utilizing expansive whitespace to signify openness and focus. Visual elements are structured to evoke a sense of tradition through classic typography, while maintaining modern accessibility through a clean, systematic layout. The emotional response should be one of "Trusted Fellowship"—a balance of professional excellence and spiritual sincerity.

## Logo

The official logo features a golden medallion with a cross, flame, and open Bible — symbols of faith, the Holy Spirit, and Scripture. The logo **must always be used with a transparent background** (PNG format). Never place the logo on a solid black or colored rectangle. When placed on dark surfaces, the gold tones provide natural contrast. On light surfaces, a subtle drop shadow may be applied to maintain legibility. The logo file should be named `logo-ocomebe.png` (transparent version).

## Colors

The palette is anchored by **Deep Navy**, representing the institutional strength and depth of faith. **Liturgical Gold** is used sparingly as a high-value accent color to denote leadership, excellence, and divine inspiration. 

- **Primary (Deep Navy):** Used for headers, primary navigation, and high-emphasis surfaces.
- **Secondary (Gold):** Reserved for primary calls to action, active states, and significant theological highlights.
- **Neutral:** A range of clean whites and cool "Silver" grays provide a breathable backdrop, ensuring the content remains the focal point.
- **Text:** Primary body copy uses a dark charcoal to maintain high legibility without the harshness of pure black.

## Typography

This design system employs a sophisticated pairings of a transitional serif for authority and a clean sans-serif for clarity.

- **Headlines:** **Source Serif 4** provides a literary, academic, and ecclesiastical feel. It should be used for all page titles, section headers, and pull quotes to reinforce the institutional voice.
- **Body & UI:** **Source Sans 3** is utilized for its exceptional legibility in long-form articles and functional UI elements. It feels modern and approachable, balancing the tradition of the serif headlines.
- **Labels:** Use uppercase styling with slight letter spacing for navigation items and category labels to distinguish them from narrative content.

## Layout & Spacing

The layout philosophy follows a **Fixed Grid** model on desktop to ensure content remains centered and readable, mimicking the layout of professional journals and traditional publications. 

- **Grid:** A 12-column grid system is used for desktop, collapsing to a single column on mobile devices.
- **Rhythm:** An 8px base unit drives all spacing. Large vertical gaps (Section Padding) are encouraged between major content blocks to convey a sense of peace and lack of clutter.
- **Margins:** Generous page margins ensure that the interface never feels "cramped," reinforcing the themes of openness and community.

## Elevation & Depth

Visual hierarchy is established primarily through **Tonal Layers** and **Low-Contrast Outlines** rather than heavy shadows. 

- **Surfaces:** Use subtle off-white or silver-gray backgrounds to differentiate cards or sections from the main page background.
- **Elevation:** When depth is required (e.g., for dropdown menus or modals), use "Soft Ambient Shadows"—very low opacity (5-8%) with a large blur radius, tinted with the Primary Navy color to keep the shadow feeling integrated with the brand.
- **Borders:** Use thin, 1px borders in `silver-leaf` to define structural elements without adding visual noise.

## Shapes

The shape language is conservative and **Soft**. The 0.25rem (4px) corner radius provides a subtle "human" touch to the interface while maintaining a structured, formal appearance. 

- **Buttons & Inputs:** Use the standard `rounded` (4px) setting.
- **Images:** Photography of congregations or clergy should use the same soft rounding to feel integrated into the design.
- **Pills:** Avoid pill-shaped buttons; stick to the soft rectangular format to maintain a professional, institutional tone.

## Components

- **Buttons:** Primary buttons use the `deep-navy` background with white text. Secondary buttons use a `silver-leaf` border with navy text. The `liturgical-gold` is reserved for high-priority actions like "Join" or "Donate."
- **Cards:** Cards should be flat with a 1px border or a very subtle tonal background. Do not use heavy shadows. Use them for news updates or pastoral resources.
- **Input Fields:** Use a clean, underlined style or a light-gray filled box with a 1px bottom border to maintain a minimalist look.
- **Lists:** Use elegant dividers between list items. For religious documents or directories, ensure ample line height for readability.
- **Navigation:** The top navigation should be clean and persistent. Use a "Mega Menu" for broad categories (Committees, Regions, Theology) to keep the structure organized and accessible.
- **Iconography:** Use thin-stroke, linear icons. Avoid filled or "playful" icon styles to preserve the professional atmosphere.
